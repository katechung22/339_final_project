Teamfight Tactics

Quick Facts:

  • Developer: Riot Games
  • Mode: Singleplayer and multiplayer
  • Genre: Team-building strategy game

Synopsis

"Teamfight Tactics (TFT) is an auto battler game developed and published by Riot Games. The game is a spinoff of League of Legends and is based on Dota Auto Chess, where players compete online against seven other opponents by building a team to be the last one standing. The game released as a League of Legends game mode for Windows and macOS in June 2019 and as a standalone game for Android and iOS in March 2020, featuring cross-platform play between them." Source: Wikipedia

Gameplay

"Based on Dota Auto Chess, a mod for Dota 2, the game centers around eight players who construct teams to fight one another and be the last player standing. The battlefield consists of hexagons, where players can strategically place units on the hexagons on their side of the game board between rounds. During each round, a short battle automatically commences, with two players matched randomly for that round, or else paired against computer-controlled enemies, frequently called PVE rounds. In the rounds against computer-controlled enemies, each enemy has a chance to drop gold, units, or items that the player can use. Health lost from losing a round is calculated with a combination of set damage per round and how many units an opponent had still alive." Source: Wikipedia

Why I Enjoy Teamfight Tactics

Teamfight Tactics was my gateway into Riot Games's ecosystem. I have always been a fan of team/deck-building games, so when this game released, I was pleasantly surprised at its gameplay, but more so, its incredible animations. I think one of the best aspects of this game is the fact that you are able to watch your team fight in real-time against other teams. This game is very dynamic and progresses where you can actively make your team stronger and stronger. I still enjoy regularly playing Teamfight Tactics and love to meet people who also have a similar passion!

Kate Chung, 2024
